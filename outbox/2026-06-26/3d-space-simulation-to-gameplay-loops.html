# 3d-space: Turn Simulation Into Gameplay Loops Explore how `/home/j/3d-space` can use its simulation layer to create richer player-facing gameplay loops. ## Goal Produce a standalone HTML report identifying concrete gameplay loops driven by server-side simulation rather than isolated scripted events. ## Candidate loops to evaluate - Dynamic trade: simulation changes supply/demand, prices, shortages, surpluses, station needs, and route opportunities. - Piracy/security: simulated cargo movement and faction/security …
Research basis
Local repository inspection of /home/j/3d-space; no external web research was used.

Executive Summary

The game can turn its simulation layer into compelling gameplay by making each simulation state imply a player action and each player action imply a later simulation change. The codebase already contains candidate loop endpoints: station markets, active jobs, feed articles, NPC piracy events, bounty hunts, conflict zones, star scanning, and discovery facets.

The design target should be small loops with clear verbs: trade, escort, scan, hunt, comply, fight, salvage, report, and choose a side. Each loop should have an immediate reward and a delayed systemic consequence.

Key Findings

  • Market loop: TradeService queries station_market_state/history and records market_trade_facts, so price/inventory trends can become jobs and route choices.
  • Piracy loop: NpcPiracyEventDriver already has demand, jettison, cargo collection, attack, disengage, and cargo pod recovery logic, making it a complete encounter loop waiting for strategic consequences.
  • Bounty loop: Feed articles can be tracked into bounty contracts, clues, lead locations, final target actors, payouts, and reputation changes.
  • Conflict loop: Faction conflict drivers can spawn faction-aligned actors, track kills/contributions, and record simulation contribution facts.
  • Exploration loop: StarScannerOverlay emits per-mode scan completions, DiscoveryManager stages star-science facets, and sale value can scale by quality.

Detailed Notes

Loop 1 — Scarcity trade. Simulation tick identifies shortage at Station A and surplus at Station B. Feed/job board publishes delivery request. Player transports goods. TradeService records market_trade_facts. Tick reduces shortage, updates route profit/risk, and emits a “shortage eased” event.

Loop 2 — Pirate pressure. Route disruptions raise pirate activity. Job board offers escort/bounty/patrol. Player encounters pirates. Compliance preserves life but feeds pirate cargo success; fighting can lower pirate pressure and improve patrol confidence; failing raises market prices and route risk.

Loop 3 — Bounty rumor. Feed article identifies a wanted pilot. Player tracks it, follows clue stages, locates the lead, scans or fights the target, and receives payout/reputation. Simulation then lowers pirate pressure or shifts faction conflict depending on target faction.

Loop 4 — Faction frontline. conflict_zones create offers for either side. Player kills or protects actors. Contributions affect control state. Feed announces victories. Stations adjust access, prices, or patrol behavior based on control.

Loop 5 — Scanner survey. Station/faction asks for optical/radio/interferometry/X-ray data. Player performs scanner minigame. Higher quality increases payout and precision. Findings unlock hazards, rare materials, companion hints, or research follow-ups.

Open Questions / Next Steps

  • For every loop, document input state → offered action → live encounter → fact output → strategic state change → UI projection.
  • Add one field to job entries: “Simulation reason,” e.g. shortage, pirate pressure, faction request, scientific anomaly.
  • Make failure outcomes intentional: lost cargo, escaped bounty, low-quality scan, or faction defeat should still update simulation and create new opportunities.
  • Favor reusable encounter modifiers over bespoke missions: route risk, faction hostility, cargo value, star hazard, and clue stage.
