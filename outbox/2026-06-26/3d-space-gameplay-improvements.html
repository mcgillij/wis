Research basis
Local repository inspection of /home/j/3d-space; no external web research was used.

Executive Summary

The project has many technically capable systems, but the biggest gameplay opportunity is to make them legible, consequential, and repeatable. The fastest improvements are not new mechanics in isolation; they are clearer feedback loops tying NPC actions, player decisions, economy state, and faction consequences together.

Focus on three player promises: “my choices changed this system,” “NPCs are doing things even when I am not watching,” and “my tools reveal new actionable options.” The current codebase already supports each promise if the systems are connected with visible status, rewards, and consequences.

Key Findings

  • Piracy is unusually complete as a scenario: raiders spawn, hail/demand, wait for jettison, collect cargo with limpets/pods, disengage, or attack. It should be surfaced as a flagship emergent encounter rather than a hidden event.
  • BountyHuntService already supports trackable feed articles, contract status, staged clues, lead locating, completion, payouts, reputation deltas, and active job entries. This can become a strong mission spine with richer on-site variation.
  • The weapons stack supports lasers, gatling, rockets, plasma, mining, limpets, probes, ammo, cooldown, heat, overheat, lock cone, projectile scenes, and multiplayer authority hooks. Combat can be deepened by adding encounter composition and target behavior rather than inventing weapon plumbing.
  • The star scanner has four modes and real input/lock mechanics. Its UX can be tuned into an exploration skill check that feeds discovery value and system knowledge.
  • ActiveJobsHudController and JobBoardPresenter are useful convergence points for “what should I do next,” but they need to show why jobs matter to factions, routes, and markets.

Detailed Notes

Recommended gameplay additions: 1) Consequence banners after live events. When a piracy event ends, show “Cargo lost: +pirate pressure on Route X,” “Raiders destroyed: bounty pressure lowered,” or “Goods recovered: station shortage relieved.” This does not require a new subsystem if event drivers write facts and ActiveJobs/Feed UI reads projections.

2) Route risk visibility. The simulation schema has trade_route_state and market_trade_facts; expose route risk and recent disruption in station offers, local map lanes, and NPC chatter. A convoy route should feel safer or more dangerous based on recent pirate pressure and patrol success.

3) NPC role readability. NpcShip already has role_tag, faction_id, cargo hints, route IDs, hostility, persona, and interaction metadata. The local map/HUD should display a compact contact card: faction, role, relation, cargo risk, current task, and whether hailing is available.

4) Combat contracts that reuse existing combat plumbing. Bounty hunts and conflict zones can vary objectives without new weapons: disable a courier before jump, protect a miner while limpets collect ore, intercept a pirate collector, scan a target before kill confirmation, or choose a side in a faction skirmish.

5) Exploration that changes jobs. Star-science facets should occasionally unlock station tasks: report high X-ray hazard, sell interferometry companion hints, find rare metallicity signatures, or flag hazardous systems for patrol/medical jobs.
