# 3d-space: Gameplay Elements Lacking Depth Audit `/home/j/3d-space` for gameplay systems that exist in some form but lack depth, meaningful player choices, progression, consequences, or systemic interaction. ## Goal Produce a standalone HTML report identifying which gameplay elements are shallow today and how to deepen them. ## Focus areas - Trading and economy depth: supply/demand, price history, production/consumption, station roles, NPC trade routes. - Combat depth: weapon variety, damage consequences, targetin…
Research basis
Local repository inspection of /home/j/3d-space; no external web research was used.

Executive Summary

The main depth gap is not lack of mechanics; it is lack of durable, player-understandable cause and effect. There are sophisticated services for simulation, combat, trading, discovery, faction conflict, bounty hunts, piracy, and star scanning, but they need tighter loops so the player perceives systemic depth instead of isolated features.

A second gap is decision pressure. Many systems can produce outcomes, but the player often needs a clearer tradeoff: comply or fight, sell cargo now or starve a market, scan safely or chase higher quality, help one faction at the expense of another, or risk a dangerous route for better profit.

Key Findings

  • Piracy has strong staged behavior but should create persistent reputation/risk/economy consequences beyond the encounter result.
  • Markets can record simulated state and player/NPC trade facts, but the player needs clearer price/inventory trend explanations and route-level causal stories.
  • Faction systems and conflict events exist, but player-facing standings, access, hostility, patrol support, and job availability should react more visibly.
  • Combat has robust weapon/damage/AI infrastructure, but encounter variety can be improved with objectives and AI roles rather than more weapon types.
  • Star scanning has real tuning mechanics, but the player needs clearer preview/reward framing: what does this mode reveal, why does quality matter, and what new action unlocks after completion?

Detailed Notes

Depth gap 1 — Cause/effect presentation. FeedEventService projects simulation events, and JobBoardPresenter builds active event/job entries. Use these as the narrative layer for systemic consequences. Every meaningful player action should produce one of: market trend, route risk, faction reputation, bounty progress, discovery value, or patrol/conflict pressure.

Depth gap 2 — Role-based NPC agency. NpcShip contains role_tag, cargo data, risk, route success/disruption counts, and hostility mode. Those fields should shape behavior and hails: miners ask for protection near belts, traders broadcast shortages, pirates select cargo-rich targets, patrols respond to distress, smugglers avoid scans, and escorts form wings.

Depth gap 3 — Reputation as access. FactionManager was present in the relevant autoload/system set; event drivers and bounty service already reference reputation deltas. Make standings unlock or lock market discounts, job tiers, landing access, patrol support, contraband tolerance, and dialogue options.

Depth gap 4 — Scanner mastery. The scanner has modes, lock thresholds, sustained-lock timing, decay, and per-mode science payloads. Add mastery by making high-quality scans produce stronger sale multipliers, more precise hazards, rare hints, or faction-specific intel requests.

Depth gap 5 — Tactical combat goals. Existing weapons include beam, gatling, rocket, plasma, probe/mining/limpet runtimes, heat, ammo, lock, and projectile authority. New depth should come from target priorities, friendly fire, disable/board/salvage choices, defensive escorts, and reinforcements.
