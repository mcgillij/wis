Research basis
Local repository inspection of /home/j/3d-space; no external web research was used.

Executive Summary

A practical roadmap should avoid sprawling new architecture. The repository already contains the key foundations: NPC simulation service, combat runtime promotion, trade persistence, conflict events, bounty service, feed projection, star-science discovery, active jobs UI, and weapon systems. The roadmap should sequence these into vertical slices that prove the game loop.

Recommended order: first make consequences visible, then connect live events to simulation facts, then deepen missions, then expand strategic ticks. This delivers player value early while reducing integration risk.

Key Findings

  • The strongest vertical slice is piracy/economy/faction because NpcPiracyEventDriver already touches dialogue, cargo, NPC AI, combat escalation, cargo drops, and completion states.
  • Bounty hunts are a good second slice because BountyHuntService already starts from feed articles, tracks clue stages, creates active jobs, and resolves rewards/reputation.
  • Faction conflict is a good third slice because conflict event drivers and simulation persistence already include contribution facts and conflict_resolved simulation events.
  • Star scanning is a fourth slice because it is comparatively self-contained and already plugs into DiscoveryManager. It can unlock exploration job variety without depending on the whole economy loop.

Detailed Notes

Phase 1: Consequence UI and fact-writing hygiene. Add a shared SimulationFactWriter, standard event-completion summaries, and a Recent Consequences feed. Instrument piracy, trade buys/sells, bounty completions, and conflict kills. Do not alter strategic rules yet; just make raw facts reliable and visible to developers.

Phase 2: Piracy route-pressure loop. On compliance, refusal, raider destruction, and cargo collection, write typed facts. Strategic tick consumes facts to update route risk and maybe station shortages/surpluses. Feed projection emits “Pirate activity rising/falling” and job board offers escorts, bounties, or anti-pirate raids.

Phase 3: Bounty/rumor loop. Wanted feed article → track bounty → clue scan/locate → target spawn → combat or scan resolution → payout/reputation → feed follow-up. Add variants where targets flee, have escorts, hide near belts, or require star/system scan clues.

Phase 4: Faction control loop. Conflict-zone contributions and NPC losses affect conflict_zones and system_control_state. The player can see frontlines, choose sides, receive faction access, and trigger patrol/reinforcement changes.

Phase 5: Exploration commerce loop. Star-science facets reveal hazards/metallicity/activity/companion hints. High-quality scans become sellable intel, station requests, faction research contracts, and map warnings.
