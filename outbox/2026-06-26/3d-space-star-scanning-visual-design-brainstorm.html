Executive Summary

The current scanner visual direction is already mode-specific: overlay drawing changes by mode, and ScannerVisionOverlay maps each mode to a post-process tint, strength, grain, bands, and quality uniform. The visual design opportunity is to make each scientific instrument feel distinct while using a consistent lock/progress language.

The best additions are layered and lightweight: mode-specific reticle motifs, quality-reactive stability, completed-mode stamps, and result cards. Avoid required external assets; the current immediate-mode drawing and shader uniforms are enough for strong iteration.

Key Findings

  • ScannerVisionOverlay maps optical to warm orange, radio to green, interferometry to cyan, and X-ray to purple with different effect_strength, grain_strength, and band_strength.
  • StarScannerOverlay draws different mode visuals: optical spectrum strip/arcs/markers, radio waterfall/pulsing arcs, interferometry waveforms/fringe-like arcs, and X-ray photon histogram/bright pulses.
  • Quality is emitted on scan_mode_changed and passed into scanner vision uniforms, so visuals can stabilize, sharpen, or brighten as the player improves lock.
  • The scanner suppresses regular HUD chrome, creating a dedicated visual moment that can support strong instrument identity.
  • All report recommendations can be implemented with dynamic Control drawing/shader uniforms; no external images or JS/assets are required.

Detailed Notes

Optical Spectroscopy concept: A horizontal spectrum strip with absorption notches drifting until wavelength/focus/gain align. Low quality shows thick, fuzzy lines and chromatic smear. High quality snaps reference and observed lines together, reduces blur, and overlays spectral class labels. Completion stamps a small “ABSORPTION PROFILE ARCHIVED” mark.

Radio Spectroscopy concept: Waterfall display with drifting bands and static. Frequency centers a bright line, bandwidth controls line thickness, squelch lowers noise. High quality reduces grain and reveals hydrogen/molecular line annotations. Use rhythmic pulses for signal center changes.

Radio Interferometry concept: Twin aperture/fringe motif. Baseline changes fringe spacing, phase aligns wave peaks, delay stabilizes correlation. High quality makes interference rings symmetrical and locks companion-hint silhouettes or off-axis ghost points.

X-Ray Spectroscopy concept: Sparse photon impacts and exposure gate. Low quality shows saturated flashes or noisy misses; high quality bins photons into a histogram and reveals flare/hazard badges. Shield bias can tint dose/safety readout.

Shared lock language: Lock ring around reticle fills only above threshold; progress bar fills during sustained lock; mode tab glows/checks when archived. Use visual shake/grain for noise and calming/stabilization for high lock.

Result card ideas: show a small instrument printout with mode icon, scan quality, revealed fields, and one actionable sentence: “High coronal activity — hazard contracts likely,” “Metal-rich signature — mining interests may pay,” or “Companion hint — follow-up interferometry recommended.”
