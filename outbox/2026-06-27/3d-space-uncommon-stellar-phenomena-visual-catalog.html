Phenomenon Notes

Wolf-Rayet Wind Bubble

Massive stripped stars with intense winds. Visual: hot blue-white core, turbulent teal/violet wind bubble, shell knots. Generate only for massive evolved stars; rare and hazardous.

Luminous Blue Variable / Hypergiant Shell

Unstable massive stars can eject shells. Visual: over-bright central star, nested translucent shells, asymmetric ejecta. Use as high-tier spectacle with strong radiation warnings.

T Tauri / Herbig Ae-Be Young System

Young stars have disks, accretion, jets, and reflection nebulosity. Visual: protoplanetary disk, bipolar jets, warm dust. Good for star-forming regions and young planets.

Protostar / Bok Globule

Embedded star formation in dark clouds. Visual: black globule silhouette with internal red glow and outflow holes. Use billboards/volumetric shells; high occlusion risk.

AGB Dusty Envelope

Late-stage cool giants lose mass and form dusty circumstellar envelopes. Visual: huge red star, smoky shell, detached arcs. Pairs well with engulfment modifiers.

Planetary Nebula

Ejected envelope ionized by hot core. Visual: bipolar lobes/rings, bright central white dwarf. Very rare and high payoff; use skybox/billboard at far range and shell meshes near.

White Dwarf Debris / Nova Remnant

WDs can accrete debris or from companions; novae create shells. Visual: tiny core, disk, knots, shell. Scan hooks: metals, radiation, recent eruption.

Pulsar / Magnetar Environment

Neutron-star beams and magnetic activity. Visual: lighthouse beams, timing rings, magnetic arcs. Use deterministic periods and hazard windows.

Black Hole / Microquasar Variant

If compact accretion already exists, add microquasar jet variant only when binary/accretion prerequisites hold. Keep visually distinct from decorative dark stars.

Cataclysmic Variable / Symbiotic Star

Mass transfer onto a white dwarf in a binary. Visual: disk, hot spot, stream from donor, flickering light. Good medium-cost binary set piece.

Contact Binary / Roche-Lobe Overflow

Two stars close enough to exchange matter or share envelopes. Visual: twin stars, figure-eight glow, curved stream. Requires binary generator support.

Be-Star Decretion Disk

Rapidly rotating B stars can have equatorial gas disks. Visual: blue star with thin glowing disk; cheap and distinctive.

Runaway-Star Bow Shock

A fast star moving through gas creates an arc shock. Visual: crescent ahead of motion, red/infrared dust glow. Useful as directional landmark.

Flare Star / Superflare System

Especially M dwarfs can produce strong flares. Visual: intermittent flares, red CME arcs, auroral planets. Low cost with strong gameplay timing.

Debris Disk / Collision Cloud

Dusty belts and collision clouds are common enough to use as uncommon modifiers. Visual: rings, resonant clumps, one-sided impact cloud. Great for mining/salvage.

Chemically Peculiar Carbon / S-Type / Barium Star

Visual distinction can be cheap: ruby carbon stars, smoky molecular bands, scan-only chemical anomalies. Good low-cost variety.

Blue Straggler in Old Cluster

A blue star that looks too young in an old population. Visual depends on context: map/scan should communicate anomaly. Use in cluster regions or faction lore.
