Individual Planet Type Catalog

1. Super-Puff / Cotton-Candy Planet

Science: Very low-density exoplanets with radii like gas giants but masses closer to ice giants or lower. CBS starting article reports two giant planets lighter than cotton candy/shaving foam; Kepler-51-like planets are the classic category.

Visual: enormous soft atmosphere shell, pastel cream/blue/pink haze, faint core shadow, slow cloud boiling. Rules: very low density, low surface gravity, young or envelope-retaining systems; vulnerable to atmospheric escape. Reliability: observed

2. Ultra-Hot Jupiter / Hot Neptune

Close-in tidally locked gas/ice giants can have daysides hot enough for ionized metals and nightside condensates. Render a blazing white/yellow dayside, dark red nightside, equatorial wind smear, and optional escaping tail.

3. Lava World / Magma Ocean Planet

Close rocky worlds or tidally heated planets can maintain molten surfaces. Use cracked black plates, bright lava seas, orange atmosphere glow, volcanic plume particles, and strong day/night contrast.

4. Carbon-Rich / Diamond Planet

Speculative but useful where disk chemistry is carbon-rich. Render graphite-dark continents, carbide deserts, amber haze, and rare crystal glints. Avoid promising literal diamond surfaces except in scan/lore hints.

5. Ocean / Hycean / Steam World

Hycean worlds are proposed hydrogen-rich planets with global oceans; ocean/steam worlds are broader plausible classes. Visuals: saturated blue-green ocean, high cloud albedo, thick atmosphere rim, pressure-ice scan layers.

6. Chthonian Planet

A stripped gas giant/Neptune core left by atmospheric escape. Visual: oversized rocky/metallic core, scorched surface, residual gas streamer, high density scan result. Good bridge between gas and rocky planet generation.

7. Rogue / Free-Floating Planet

Free-floating planets are observed by microlensing/surveys. In a game, make them dark travel landmarks with geothermal cracks, aurora from interstellar plasma, and possible ice-shell ocean.

8. Circumbinary / High-Eccentricity / Resonant Planet

The rarity is orbital architecture more than material. Visual identity comes from double shadows, shifting seasons, alternating star colors, and orbital UI traces.

9. Pulsar / White-Dwarf / Compact-Remnant Planet

Pulsar planets are real in at least some systems. The provided SpaceDaily/Webb-style source reports a lemon-shaped planet around a pulsar with a carbon atmosphere; treat that specific claim as requiring primary-paper verification before hard canon. In-game visual: tidally stretched silhouette, carbon haze, radiation-battered aurora, and compact-remnant lighting.

10. Helium Planet

A gas/ice planet that has lost hydrogen preferentially can become helium-enriched. Visualize pale gray/yellow atmosphere, weak methane colors, and strong escape stream if close to the star.

11. Evaporating / Disintegrating Planet

Close-in planets can lose atmosphere or dust. Render comet-like tails, sputtering particles, transit dimming scan text, and a small body that looks unstable.

12. Iron-Rich Super-Mercury

High-density planets may be mantle-stripped or formed iron-rich. Visual: dark metallic basalt, huge impact basin, high radar reflectivity, sparse sodium/calcium exosphere.

13. Ring-Dominant Super-Planet

Large ring systems are visually powerful and physically plausible under stability constraints. Use ring shadows, shepherd-moon gaps, and strong scan/map silhouette. Keep extremely huge rings rare and dynamically justified.

14. Mini-Neptune / Sub-Neptune Ambiguous World

Common in exoplanet data but uncommon in Solar System terms. Visual: thick atmosphere over uncertain surface, muted blue/gray palette, pressure-haze shell. Use as a base for Hycean, helium, and evaporating modifiers.

15. Lemon / Roche-Distorted Compact-Remnant World

For the reported Webb case, the game-facing lesson is valuable even if the exact article needs verification: compact remnant planets can be visually weird. Render non-spherical scale with shader normal distortion or mesh squash along the star direction, plus carbon-rich atmosphere and extreme radiation warnings.

Procedural Generation Rules

  • Represent most rare planets as planet_class plus rare_modifier: e.g. gas_giant + super_puff, rocky + lava_world, mini_neptune + hycean.
  • Gate by host star, orbital distance, age, metallicity, and prior system history. Example: chthonian requires close orbit and atmospheric-loss history; rogue requires no normal host context.
  • Keep mutually exclusive rules: super-puff and iron-rich core should not stack; Hycean and lava should not stack; compact-remnant lemon world should not appear around ordinary G stars.
  • Expose scan confidence: observed, strongly plausible, speculative-but-useful.

Godot Implementation Notes

  • Super-puffs: large transparent atmosphere shell, soft depth fade, low-detail core; excellent billboard/impostor candidate.
  • Ultra-hot planets: day/night material blend driven by star direction; emissive dayside and wind smear noise.
  • Lava worlds: cracked mask texture + animated emissive lava; optional plume particles.
  • Rings/tails: separate mesh/particle components so they can be LOD-disabled.
  • Compact-remnant distortion: start with non-uniform scale and normal-map directionality before custom mesh deformation.
