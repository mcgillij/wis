Executive Summary

The strongest additions for 3d-space are not new base star classes but rare system-level modifiers layered onto normal stars: polluted giant, recently engulfed planet, warm dust aftermath, metal-polluted white dwarf, active Roche stream, and disintegrating rocky planet. They tell a story at a glance: something fell in, was torn apart, or is still feeding the star.

Make these rare, scan-forward discoveries. The visuals should combine a modified star material, a sky/backdrop billboard for large-scale dust or shell structure, and local meshes/particles for rings, debris arcs, and accretion streams.

Individual Candidate Notes

1. Engulfed-Planet Polluted Giant

Science basis: As stars expand off the main sequence, close-in planets can spiral in. Observable hints include abundance anomalies, enhanced rotation, dust, and orbital architecture with missing inner planets. The starting Universe Today article reports stellar evidence consistent with engulfment; treat individual cases as evidence-weighted, not a directly visible swallowed planet.

Visual identity: swollen amber/red star, slightly mottled surface, off-axis dust plume, cleared inner-orbit “scar,” and short-lived ember fragments. Use a warm orange/gold palette with darker brown dust.

Rules: requires subgiant/red giant/AGB; requires prior inner planet; rare; mutually exclusive with pristine stable inner rocky system. Reliability: strongly plausible

2. Lithium-Rich Giant

Observed lithium-rich giants are real, but the cause can be internal production or planet engulfment. In-game, make this a scan anomaly rather than a guaranteed story. Visualize with subtle spectral-line UI, increased activity, and unusual golden-green corona tint.

3. Rapid-Rotator Post-Engulfment Star

A swallowed planet can transfer angular momentum. Render fast surface noise advection, broader equatorial halo, oblate-looking flare distribution, and frequent coronal mass ejection particles. Good low-cost modifier for F/G/K subgiants.

4. Warm Dust Shell / Recent Collision Aftermath

Infrared excess can reveal warm circumstellar dust from collisions or disruptions. Use broken torus meshes, billboard clumps, and orange backscatter. Make the dust denser on one orbital arc to imply recent origin.

5. Metal-Polluted White Dwarf with Debris Disk

White dwarfs often show atmospheric metals that should sink quickly, implying ongoing/recent accretion of rocky debris. This is one of the best science-grounded visuals: small intense white star, thin dusty/gaseous disk, tidal fragments inside the Roche limit.

6. Tidal Planet Disruption / Roche Stream

For compact stars or evolved giants, a close body can be stretched into a stream. Use a Bezier ribbon/particle path feeding the star, with denser knots and hot impact spot. This is visually high-value but should be rare and performance-tiered.

7. Disintegrating Rocky Planet

Ultra-short-period rocky planets can shed dusty tails. Render as a small dark body with a comet-like tail, intermittent chunks, and transit-dimming scan text. Great as a local discovery near hot stars.

8. Giant Impact Dust Cloud

A recent planet-planet collision can generate large warm dust clouds. Visualize a crescent cloud in one orbital lane, multiple remnant chunks, and a scan reading of silicate vapor/dust.

9. Star-Planet Magnetic Interaction

Close giant planets around active stars may couple magnetically. Render looping plasma lines from star toward the planet, pulsed auroral footprint on the stellar surface, and synchronized flare timing.

10. Nova Shell / Cataclysmic Variable Aftermath

Accreting white dwarfs in binaries can erupt, creating shells and knots. This is adjacent to engulfment but visually valuable. Use a central binary, expanding translucent shell, and high-radiation hazard.

11. Exocomet Storm

Falling evaporating bodies around young or disturbed stars create absorption signatures and dust. Render many small faint tails, all roughly anti-star, with scan rewards for volatiles.

12. Fallback / Remnant Disk

Some compact remnants may have fallback material. In-game, reserve for rare compact systems: dark disk, hot inner rim, forbidden salvage, and unstable navigation.

Planet Engulfment Event Chain

  1. Pre-engulfment: close-in planet has decaying orbit; star shows strong tides, magnetic arcs, and increasing flare rate.
  2. Active disruption: Roche stream, evaporating tail, hot impact footprint, transient optical/infrared brightening. This should be extremely rare or scripted because it is short-lived.
  3. Immediate aftermath: warm dust shell, asymmetric plume, missing planet, unstable fragments, elevated activity.
  4. Long-term polluted state: abundance anomaly, spin-up, altered remaining orbits, remnant debris belt; best for procedural rarity because it persists longer.

Godot Implementation Notes

  • Low cost: star shader parameter modifiers, billboard dust arcs, scan text, color ramp shifts.
  • Medium cost: ring/disk mesh, GPU particles for fragments, curved ribbon stream, local debris props.
  • High cost: volumetric shells, animated Roche streams with collision avoidance, large multi-layer nebula backdrops.
  • Use deterministic modifier stacks: base star class + rare aftermath modifier + optional companion debris modifier.
  • Render far-view state as skybox/billboard silhouettes; promote to local rings/particles only when the player enters the affected system.
