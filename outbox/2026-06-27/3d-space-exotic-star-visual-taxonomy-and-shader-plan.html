Executive Summary

Recommended new exotic / unusual centerpiece visuals for 3d-space: white dwarf debris star, brown dwarf ember, magnetar, pulsar lighthouse, Wolf-Rayet wind star, carbon star, and protostar / T Tauri disk system. Treat quark stars, strange stars, preon stars, gravastars, and Thorne-Żytkow objects as later speculative variants or lore anomalies unless the game wants intentionally weird science-fantasy.

Per-Type Visual Briefs

White Dwarf

  • Palette: white, blue-white, pale cyan; optional red/orange disk rim.
  • Silhouette: tiny intense core with sharp bloom, little surface texture, high contrast against dark sky.
  • Shader features: small sphere, strong emissive falloff, hard limb, thin accretion/debris disk variant.
  • Difference from neutron star: calmer, no beams by default, larger/less extreme hazard, more “dead sun” than cosmic weapon.

Brown Dwarf

  • Palette: dark red, magenta, amber, infrared-feeling low saturation.
  • Silhouette: gas-giant-like bands with faint internal glow and weak corona.
  • Shader features: rotating cloud bands, noise storms, minimal bloom, strong nearby darkness.
  • Role: low-light exploration systems, hidden stations, cold moons.

Magnetar

  • Palette: electric blue, white, violet, occasional red flare warning.
  • Silhouette: compact core wrapped by twisted magnetic loop ribbons and burst particles.
  • Animation: irregular flare spikes; avoid perfectly steady pulsar rhythm.
  • Hazards: radiation bursts, sensor overload, navigation disruption.

Pulsar

  • Palette: blue-white core, cyan beam cones, dark transparent timing rings.
  • Silhouette: two opposed beams sweeping with stable period; optional equatorial wind nebula.
  • Shader features: beam mesh cones with additive material, rotating parent, pulse-synchronized bloom.
  • Role: readable from far away; can become navigation/timing puzzle.

Wolf-Rayet Star

  • Palette: hot blue/white core, teal/violet wind shell, pale emission knots.
  • Silhouette: thick wind halo, blown bubble, high mass-loss streaks.
  • Difference from O star: more turbulent, stripped, with visible wind shell and stronger environmental effect.

Carbon Star

  • Palette: deep ruby, burnt orange, smoky brown, occasional soot-dark molecular bands.
  • Silhouette: large cool giant with soft dusty halo; slow surface motion.
  • Role: non-compact exotic-looking regular/evolved star; useful because it is cheap and visually distinct.

Protostar / T Tauri / Herbig Ae-Be

  • Palette: warm young star, dark dusty disk, blue bipolar jets, reflection nebula.
  • Silhouette: central object partly hidden by disk/envelope; jets give strong axis.
  • Scene composition: disk mesh + two jet particle beams + backdrop dust billboards.

Speculative Compact Objects

Quark/strange stars, preon stars, and gravastars are best as ultra-rare late-game variants of the neutron/dark-star scene family. Use different surface/halo language—crystalline flicker, gravitational mirage, or impossible lens bands—while clearly labeling them speculative in scan text.

Reusable Godot Shader / Scene Components

  • StarCoreSphere: emissive sphere with spectral color, limb darkening, surface noise speed, spot density, and bloom multiplier.
  • CoronaShell: one or more transparent additive shells; parameters for thickness, filament scale, wind direction, and pulse response.
  • DiskComponent: ring mesh for debris/accretion/protoplanetary disks; expose inner radius, outer radius, warp, dust opacity, and hot rim color.
  • BeamComponent: cone/cylinder meshes for pulsars, jets, and magnetar bursts; expose period, phase, width, and hazard coupling.
  • BillboardHeroImpostor: far-view version for map/backdrop consistency, using the same palette and silhouette metadata.

Implementation Pitfalls

  • Do not make every rare object brighter than every normal star; brown dwarfs and white dwarfs should be distinctive via contrast and context, not just bloom.
  • Differentiate pulsar vs magnetar: pulsar is periodic and geometric; magnetar is bursty, distorted, and unstable.
  • Keep black hole visuals unique: lensing/accretion dominance belongs primarily there, not on every compact object.
  • Expose LOD: far impostor, mid billboard, near mesh/particle stack.

Roadmap

  1. First: white dwarf, brown dwarf, pulsar/magnetar variants because they reuse compact-star infrastructure and far impostor tests.
  2. Second: Wolf-Rayet and carbon star because they broaden stellar color/scale vocabulary.
  3. Third: protostar/T Tauri systems once disk/jet components are robust.
  4. Later: TZO, quark/strange star, gravastar/preon as very rare lore anomalies.
